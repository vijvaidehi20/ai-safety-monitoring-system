Suspicious Behavior Detection Guidelines

Certain behavioral patterns may indicate a potential safety risk.

Indicators of suspicious activity include:
- A person suddenly falling or collapsing.
- Rapid or repeated distress gestures.
- A group of individuals surrounding a single person.
- Aggressive body posture such as raised arms or pushing motions.

If suspicious behavior occurs in combination with environmental risk factors such as poor lighting or low crowd density, the overall risk level increases.

Repeated distress gestures within a short time frame should be treated as potential calls for help.
