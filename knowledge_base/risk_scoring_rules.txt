Risk Scoring Guidelines

Base risk score: 0

Environmental Risk Factors
Add 2 points:
- If event happens during night hours (20:00–05:00)
- If lighting conditions are low or visibility is poor

Behavioral Risk Factors
Add 3 points:
- If sudden fall or collapse is detected
- If aggressive body posture or threatening gestures are detected

Crowd Dynamics
Add 2 points:
- If crowd count is greater than 2 within a 2–3 meter radius

Add 3 additional points:
- If crowd is surrounding a single individual

Situational Escalation
Add 2 points:
- If the detected person shows repeated distress gestures
- If the incident continues for more than 10 seconds

Risk Levels
0–2 → LOW (Normal activity)
3–5 → MEDIUM (Suspicious situation)
6–8 → HIGH (Potential safety threat)
9+ → CRITICAL (Immediate intervention required)

Important Reasoning Rule:
Risk assessment should consider behavioral signals, environmental conditions, and crowd dynamics together rather than evaluating them independently.
